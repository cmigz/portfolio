About Me

Hey, welcome to my portfolio.
My name is Christian.

Coder | Entrepreneur | Gamer

I've always been a very methodical and analytical person. When I discovered my passion and ability for coding I realized I had found what I was meant to do in this life.

I consider myself a very well rounded developer. As much as I love designing a beautiful and responsive front-end with a smooth UI I have the same vigor for building databases and creating API's so my clients don't get so lonely and have someone to talk to.

My personal philosophy focuses on two key aspects of life. Constantly strive for self-improvement, and always treat others as you'd want to be treated yourself. I come from a hard-working old-school Italian family where going above beyond is the standard.

Ok, that's enough self-aggrandizing for now. Let me step away from 'Christian' the developer and tell you about the person.

All my life I've been a gamer. It's not even the act alone that keeps me captivated, its the world of gaming and it's culture. I also have a great appreciation of good television, by that I mean the writing, the acting, all of it. If you've gotten this far it should come as no surprise that I'm also a huge comic book aficionado. Would you believe me if I told you Alan Moore's 1998 interpretation of "The Swamp Thing" is one of the best love stories of all time? Trust me.

One of the proudest personal accomplishments is I can say that when I lost my leg at the age of 13 I taught myself how to walk again. I was young, stubborn, and so lost with such a drastic shift of my status quo. But the fact is I consider that to be one of the greatest things to ever happen to me. Had I not gone through that I would not have come out the other side being the thankful, empathetic, badass that I am today.

Chris Turco Law

- Primary Tech -
HTML | CSS | JavaScript

Chris Turco Law is where my coding journey began. A friend had recently passed the bar and was opening his own firm. I told him he'd need a website, having no idea where to start he asked if I would set him up one. At the time I'd had close to no exposure to web development. I built the first iteration of the site using WordPress and learning some HTML/CSS to customize some of the their plugins.

After completing GA's Web Development Immersive program I re-tackled the project with all the information I'd learned and started it over from scratch. Using a marketing template for the layout of out landing and keeping speed in mind you'll see the newer version (linked in the button below) runs much faster and has a better flow then the original version.

Source code is in a private repository which I'd be happy to share upon request.
